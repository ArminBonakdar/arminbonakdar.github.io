About Me

I am Armin Bonakdar, a second-year Master's student at the University of Alberta. Currently, I am doing my Master's degree at the Neuromuscular Control and Biomechanics Lab (NCBLab) at the University of Alberta under the supervision of Dr. Hossein Rouhani and Dr. Mahdi Tavakoli. I am primarily focused on biomechanics, mechatronics, robotics, and machine learning.

My passion lies in exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence, mechatronics, and biomechanics. I enjoy tackling complex problems.

Education

I got my bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering at K. N. Toosi University in Iran with a GPA of 18.36 out of 20. During my undergraduate education, I worked as a research assistant at a prestigious biomedical engineering center in one of Iran's leading hospitals, providing me with valuable insights into real-world applications of my research.

I am now, pursuing my Master's at University of Alberta. I finished my Master's courses and maintained a GPA of 4 out of 4. I am primarily focused on biomechanics, mechatronics, robotics, and machine learning. Throughout grad school, I have been involved in different projects. One of them is evaluating industrial exoskeletons using Electromyography (EMG) and Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs). I have also explored muscular fatigue detection using wearable sensors with deep learning algorithms.
